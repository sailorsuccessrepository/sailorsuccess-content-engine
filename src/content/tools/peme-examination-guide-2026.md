# PEME Medical Examination for Seafarers 2026: Complete Guide

The Pre-Employment Medical Examination (PEME) is a mandatory health assessment that every seafarer must pass before joining a vessel. This comprehensive examination ensures you're physically and mentally fit for the demanding conditions at sea.

## What is PEME?

PEME stands for Pre-Employment Medical Examination. It's a standardized health check required by:
- Shipping companies before employment
- Flag states for certification
- P&I clubs for insurance
- International maritime regulations (STCW, MLC 2006)

## Types of Medical Examinations

### 1. Company PEME
- Conducted before each contract
- Valid for specific company joining
- More comprehensive than flag state medical
- Follows company/P&I club requirements

### 2. Flag State Medical
- For Certificate of Competency renewal
- Valid for 2 years (5 years if under 18)
- Follows flag state requirements
- Basic fitness assessment

### 3. ILO/MLC Medical
- As per MLC 2006 requirements
- Standardized across countries
- Valid for 2 years
- Includes eyesight and hearing standards

## PEME Tests and Examinations

### Physical Examination

| Assessment | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| Height & Weight | BMI calculation |
| Blood Pressure | Normal: <140/90 mmHg |
| Pulse | Resting heart rate |
| General Physical | Overall health assessment |
| Musculoskeletal | Mobility, strength, flexibility |
| Respiratory | Lung function, breathing |
| Cardiovascular | Heart sounds, circulation |
| Neurological | Reflexes, sensation |

### Laboratory Tests

**Blood Tests:**
| Test | Purpose |
|------|---------|
| Complete Blood Count | Overall health |
| Blood Sugar (Fasting) | Diabetes screening |
| HbA1c | Long-term blood sugar |
| Lipid Profile | Cholesterol levels |
| Liver Function | Liver health |
| Kidney Function | Renal health |
| HIV Test | Infectious disease |
| HBsAg | Hepatitis B |
| VDRL | Syphilis screening |

**Urine Tests:**
- Routine examination
- Drug screening (some companies)
- Albumin and sugar

### Specialized Tests

**Vision Test:**
| Parameter | Deck Officers | Engine Officers |
|-----------|--------------|-----------------|
| Unaided Vision | 6/6 or 6/9 | 6/12 acceptable |
| Corrected Vision | 6/6 required | 6/9 required |
| Color Vision | Essential | Required |
| Night Vision | Required | - |
| Field of Vision | Full | Required |

**Hearing Test:**
- Audiometry at 500, 1000, 2000, 4000 Hz
- Maximum hearing loss: 25-30 dB
- Each ear tested separately

**Cardiac Tests:**
| Age | Required Tests |
|-----|---------------|
| Below 40 | ECG |
| 40-50 | ECG + Stress test (if indicated) |
| Above 50 | ECG + Stress/Echo |

**Other Tests:**
- Chest X-ray (PA view)
- Pulmonary function test
- Eye examination (fundoscopy)
- Dental examination (some companies)

## Medical Fitness Standards

### STCW Vision Standards

**Deck Department:**
- Distance vision: 6/6 in one eye, 6/12 in other
- Near vision: Able to read standard text
- Color vision: Must pass Ishihara test
- Night blindness: Not acceptable

**Engine Department:**
- Distance vision: 6/9 in better eye
- Near vision: Reading ability required
- Color vision: Ability to distinguish colors

### Conditions That May Cause Issues

**Generally Disqualifying:**
1. Uncontrolled diabetes (HbA1c > 8.5%)
2. Epilepsy or seizure disorders
3. Severe mental health conditions
4. Substance abuse disorders
5. Certain cardiac conditions
6. HIV positive (some flag states)
7. Active tuberculosis
8. Color blindness (deck officers)

**May Require Clearance:**
1. Controlled diabetes
2. Controlled hypertension
3. History of depression (controlled)
4. Previous surgeries
5. Mild hearing loss
6. Corrected vision
7. BMI > 35

### BMI Requirements

| BMI Range | Status |
|-----------|--------|
| 18.5 - 25 | Normal - Fit |
| 25 - 30 | Overweight - Usually fit |
| 30 - 35 | Obese - May need clearance |
| > 35 | Severely obese - Often unfit |
| < 18.5 | Underweight - Assessment needed |

## PEME Procedure

### Before Examination

**Preparation:**
1. Get 8 hours sleep night before
2. Fast for 10-12 hours (blood tests)
3. Avoid alcohol for 48 hours
4. Stay hydrated (water allowed)
5. Bring all required documents
6. Carry existing medical records

**Documents Required:**
- Passport
- CDC
- Previous medical reports
- Company nomination letter
- Previous PEME reports
- Vaccination records
- Prescription if on medication

### During Examination

**Typical Schedule (4-6 hours):**
| Time | Procedure |
|------|-----------|
| 0800 | Registration, vital signs |
| 0830 | Blood and urine samples |
| 0900 | Vision and hearing tests |
| 0930 | ECG and chest X-ray |
| 1000 | Physical examination |
| 1030 | Doctor consultation |
| 1100 | Additional tests if needed |
| 1200 | Report collection (or next day) |

### After Examination

**Results Timeline:**
- Fit Certificate: Same day or next day
- Referral: If additional tests needed
- Unfit: Written reasons provided
- Appeal: Process available

## Common Reasons for Unfit Declaration

### Medical Issues
1. **Diabetes:** HbA1c > 8.5%, uncontrolled
2. **Hypertension:** BP > 160/100 persistent
3. **Obesity:** BMI > 40
4. **Heart conditions:** Arrhythmias, previous MI
5. **Vision:** Below standard, color blindness
6. **Hearing:** Significant loss

### Lifestyle Issues
1. Drug test positive
2. Alcohol markers elevated
3. Recent injury not healed
4. Recent surgery recovery

## Tips to Pass PEME

### Long-term Preparation

**6 Months Before:**
- Start regular exercise
- Improve diet
- Reduce alcohol consumption
- Quit smoking
- Monitor blood pressure
- Control blood sugar

**3 Months Before:**
- Health checkup to identify issues
- Address any medical problems
- Lose weight if needed
- Get proper treatment

**1 Month Before:**
- Verify documents ready
- Confirm appointment
- Continue healthy lifestyle
- Get adequate rest

### Immediate Preparation

**1 Week Before:**
- Avoid strenuous exercise
- Get regular sleep
- Stay hydrated
- Avoid new medications
- Reduce stress

**Day Before:**
- Light dinner
- Start fasting at prescribed time
- Prepare documents
- Sleep early

**Examination Day:**
- Arrive early
- Stay calm
- Honest about medical history
- Follow instructions

## Dealing with Medical Issues

### If Found Temporarily Unfit
1. Get detailed written report
2. Consult specialist for treatment
3. Follow prescribed treatment
4. Re-examination after treatment
5. Obtain clearance letter

### If Found Permanently Unfit
1. Get second opinion
2. Understand specific reason
3. Explore alternative careers
4. Consult maritime medical specialist
5. Check if appeal possible

### Managing Chronic Conditions

**Diabetes:**
- Maintain HbA1c < 7%
- Regular monitoring
- Carry specialist letter
- Proper medication

**Hypertension:**
- Keep BP < 140/90
- Consistent medication
- Lifestyle modifications
- Cardiologist clearance

## Frequently Asked Questions

### How long is PEME valid?
Typically 3 months for joining, but varies by company. Flag state medical valid 2 years.

### Can I fail PEME for being overweight?
Yes, if BMI > 35-40 depending on company/P&I requirements. Weight loss recommended.

### What if I wear glasses?
Glasses are acceptable if corrected vision meets standards. Carry spare glasses onboard.

### Can I get PEME done anywhere?
No, must be at company/P&I approved clinic. Check approved list before booking.

### What if I'm on regular medication?
Disclose all medications. Some conditions are acceptable with proper control and documentation.
