# Yellow Fever Vaccination for Seafarers: Complete Guide 2026

Yellow fever vaccination is a mandatory requirement for seafarers traveling to endemic regions. Without valid vaccination certificates, you may be denied port entry, quarantined, or refused boarding. This guide covers everything Indian seafarers need to know about yellow fever vaccination requirements.

## What is Yellow Fever?

### Understanding the Disease

Yellow fever is a viral hemorrhagic disease transmitted by infected mosquitoes. It occurs primarily in tropical regions of Africa and South America.

**Key Facts:**
| Aspect | Details |
|--------|---------|
| Transmission | Aedes mosquito bite |
| Incubation | 3-6 days |
| Endemic Areas | Africa, South America |
| Prevention | Vaccination |
| Severity | Can be fatal (15-50% mortality in severe cases) |

### Why Seafarers Need Vaccination

As a seafarer, you may:
- Visit endemic countries
- Transit through endemic ports
- Be required by flag state regulations
- Need it for immigration clearance
- Face quarantine without it

### International Certificate of Vaccination (ICV)

**Yellow Card Details:**
| Field | Information |
|-------|-------------|
| Personal Details | Name, date of birth, nationality |
| Vaccine Details | Type, manufacturer, batch number |
| Date of Vaccination | Day/month/year |
| Validity | Lifetime (since 2016 amendment) |
| Center Stamp | Official seal |
| Doctor Signature | Administering physician |

### Validity Changes

**Important Update (2016):**
- Previously: Certificate valid for 10 years
- Now: Valid for LIFE of the person
- WHO amendment effective July 11, 2016
- Older certificates automatically lifetime valid

## Side Effects and Precautions

### Common Side Effects

**Mild Reactions (10-30%):**
- Soreness at injection site
- Low-grade fever
- Headache
- Muscle aches
- Fatigue

**Duration:** 1-3 days, self-limiting

### Serious Reactions (Rare)

| Reaction | Risk | Population |
|----------|------|------------|
| Allergic reaction | 1 in 131,000 | All |
| Neurologic disease | 1 in 125,000 | All |
| Viscerotropic disease | 1 in 250,000 | Elderly, immunocompromised |

### Who Should NOT Get Vaccinated

**Contraindications:**
1. **Allergy**: Egg or vaccine component allergy
2. **Age**: Infants under 9 months
3. **Immune Compromise**: HIV/AIDS with low CD4, chemotherapy
4. **Thymus Disorders**: Past thymus problems
5. **Elderly**: Caution over 60 (first-time vaccination)

### Medical Exemption

**If You Cannot Be Vaccinated:**
- Get medical waiver letter
- Carry from WHO-approved center
- May still face quarantine in some countries
- Discuss alternatives with ship medical officer

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Q: Can I join ship without yellow fever certificate?

**A:** Depends on vessel's trading area. If visiting endemic regions, NO. Many companies require it regardless as a precaution.

### Q: My certificate is from before 2016. Is it still valid?

**A:** Yes! All certificates issued before July 2016 are now valid for life, not just 10 years.

### Q: What if I'm allergic to eggs?

**A:** Discuss with doctor. You may get a medical exemption letter, but this may limit your port access in endemic countries.

### Q: How long before joining should I get vaccinated?

**A:** Minimum 10 days, ideally 2-4 weeks. The certificate is only valid from 10 days post-vaccination.

### Q: Is the vaccine safe?

**A:** Yes, with very rare serious side effects. For healthy individuals, benefits far outweigh risks.

### Q: Will company pay for vaccination?

**A:** Most reputable companies reimburse. Confirm with your manning agent.
