# Merchant Navy Medical Test 2026: Complete Guide

Your maritime career depends on passing the medical examination. Every year, thousands of aspirants are rejected due to medical issues they could have addressed earlier. This guide helps you pass on your first attempt.

## Why Medical Fitness Matters

At sea, you're far from hospitals. Ships need crew who can:
- Handle emergencies
- Work in confined spaces
- Climb ladders and stairs
- Stand long watches
- Survive in lifeboats if needed

## Types of Medical Examinations

### Pre-Sea Medical

Required before joining maritime training:
- For institute admission
- Basic fitness check
- Can be repeated if failed

### CDC Medical

Required for obtaining CDC:
- More comprehensive
- DG Shipping standards
- Valid for 2 years

### Periodic Medical

Required every 2 years:
- Renewal of fitness certificate
- Same standards as CDC medical
- For serving seafarers

## Tests Conducted

### Vision Tests

| Test | Requirement (Deck) | Requirement (Engine) |
|------|-------------------|---------------------|
| Distance Vision | 6/6 both eyes | 6/12 both eyes |
| Near Vision | N5 | N5 |
| Color Vision | Normal | Normal |
| Night Vision | Normal | Normal |
| Field of Vision | Full | Full |

**Color Vision Tests:**
- Ishihara plates (primary)
- Lantern test (if needed)
- Anomaloscope (final confirmation)

### Hearing Tests

| Parameter | Requirement |
|-----------|-------------|
| Pure Tone Audiometry | Normal range |
| Speech Recognition | Clear at normal volume |
| Whisper Test | Hear at 2 meters |

### Physical Examination

**Height and Weight:**
- Minimum height: 157 cm (deck), 150 cm (engine)
- BMI: 18-30 (healthy range)

**Cardiovascular:**
- Blood pressure: Normal range
- ECG: Normal
- No heart conditions

**Respiratory:**
- Chest X-ray: Clear
- Lung function: Normal
- No asthma (severe)

**Musculoskeletal:**
- Full range of motion
- No major deformities
- Grip strength adequate

### Laboratory Tests

| Test | Purpose |
|------|---------|
| Blood Sugar | Diabetes screening |
| Blood Count | General health |
| Urine Analysis | Kidney function |
| HIV Test | Mandatory |
| Drug Test | Substance screening |

### Psychological Assessment

- Mental stability evaluation
- Stress handling capability
- No psychiatric conditions

## Disqualifying Conditions

### Absolute Disqualifications

These conditions will permanently disqualify you:

| Condition | Reason |
|-----------|--------|
| Complete color blindness | Navigation safety |
| Severe epilepsy | Emergency response |
| Insulin-dependent diabetes | Medical access |
| Active tuberculosis | Contagious |
| HIV (symptomatic) | Health risk |
| Major psychiatric disorders | Safety concern |
| Severe heart conditions | Physical demands |

### Conditional Disqualifications

May be considered case-by-case:

| Condition | Possibility |
|-----------|-------------|
| Controlled diabetes | With medical review |
| Mild asthma | With medication plan |
| Corrected vision | With glasses |
| Past surgery | After recovery |
| Controlled BP | With documentation |

## How to Prepare

### 3 Months Before

**Vision Care:**
- Get comprehensive eye check
- Address any issues
- Start eye exercises
- Reduce screen time

**Physical Fitness:**
- Start regular exercise
- Build cardiovascular health
- Achieve healthy weight
- Quit smoking

**Health Habits:**
- Regular sleep schedule
- Balanced diet
- Reduce alcohol
- No recreational drugs

### 1 Week Before

**Immediate Preparation:**
- Get adequate sleep
- Stay hydrated
- Avoid alcohol completely
- No heavy exercise (avoid injury)
- Eat light, healthy meals

### Day Before

**Final Preparation:**
- Sleep early (8 hours)
- Light dinner
- No alcohol or caffeine
- Prepare documents
- Set alarm

### Examination Day

**Morning:**
- Light breakfast
- Arrive early
- Carry all documents
- Stay calm
- Dress comfortably

## Documents Required

1. Application form
2. Passport size photos (4)
3. ID proof (Aadhar/Passport)
4. Previous medical records
5. Payment receipt

## Common Reasons for Failure

### Vision Problems

**Issues:**
- Uncorrected myopia
- Color vision deficiency
- Night blindness

**Solutions:**
- Get LASIK/glasses early
- Color blindness: limited options
- Address before attempting

### Weight Issues

**Issues:**
- Overweight (BMI >30)
- Underweight (BMI <18)

**Solutions:**
- Diet and exercise program
- Consult nutritionist
- Allow 3-6 months

### Blood Pressure

**Issues:**
- High BP uncontrolled
- Anxiety-induced spike

**Solutions:**
- Get it checked regularly
- Lifestyle modifications
- Medication if needed
- Practice relaxation

### Blood Sugar

**Issues:**
- Pre-diabetes
- Undiagnosed diabetes

**Solutions:**
- Regular monitoring
- Diet control
- Exercise
- Medical management

## If You Fail the Medical

### Immediate Steps

1. Get detailed report
2. Understand the reason
3. Consult specialist
4. Create action plan

### Appeal Process

If you believe result is wrong:
1. Request retest
2. Provide additional evidence
3. Get specialist opinion
4. Submit appeal to DG Shipping

### Alternative Careers

If permanently disqualified:
- Shore-based maritime jobs
- Port management
- Shipping logistics
- Maritime law
- Marine surveying

## Special Cases

### LASIK Surgery

**Requirements:**
- Wait 6 months after surgery
- Stable vision
- Approval from ophthalmologist
- May need additional tests

### Previous Injuries

**Requirements:**
- Full recovery documentation
- Specialist clearance
- Functional capability proof
- Case-by-case evaluation

## Conclusion

The medical examination is not designed to fail you—it's designed to ensure you're fit for sea service. Most issues are preventable or manageable with early attention.

Key points:
- Start preparation early
- Address known issues
- Get pre-checked before official exam
- Maintain healthy lifestyle
- Don't attempt if not ready

Your health is your career asset. Invest in it.
