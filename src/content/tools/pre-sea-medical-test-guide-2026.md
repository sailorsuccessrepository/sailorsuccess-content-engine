# Pre-Sea Medical Test for Merchant Navy 2026

**Your medical fitness determines your maritime career.** The pre-sea medical examination is one of the first hurdles every merchant navy aspirant must clear. Fail this, and your sea career stops before it begins. This guide covers everything you need to know.

## Why Pre-Sea Medical is Mandatory

Life at sea is demanding. Seafarers work in isolated environments where:

- Medical facilities are limited
- Emergency evacuation is difficult and expensive
- Physical tasks are demanding
- Mental health challenges are common

The pre-sea medical ensures:
1. You're fit to handle shipboard duties
2. You won't endanger yourself or crew
3. You can complete contract without medical repatriation

## Medical Standards Overview

The medical examination follows **ILO/IMO Guidelines for Seafarer Medical Examination** and DG Shipping standards.

### Physical Requirements

| Parameter | Requirement |
|-----------|-------------|
| Vision (unaided) | 6/6 in one eye, 6/9 in other (Deck) |
| Vision (corrected) | 6/6 in both eyes (Deck), 6/12 (Engine) |
| Color Vision | Normal (Deck officers mandatory) |
| Hearing | Normal conversation at 2 meters |
| BMI Range | 18.5 - 30 (ideal: 20-25) |
| Blood Pressure | Below 140/90 mmHg |

### Conditions That May Disqualify

**Automatic Disqualification:**
- Active tuberculosis
- HIV/AIDS
- Epilepsy
- Severe mental disorders
- Uncontrolled diabetes
- Heart diseases requiring medication
- Color blindness (for deck officers)

**May Require Assessment:**
- Corrected vision
- Controlled diabetes
- Minor surgeries
- Skin conditions
- Past medical history

## Tests Conducted in Pre-Sea Medical

### 1. Vision Tests

**Visual Acuity Test:**
- Snellen chart reading
- Both eyes tested separately
- With and without glasses

**Color Vision Test:**
- Ishihara plates (24 plates)
- Lantern test (if Ishihara inconclusive)
- Critical for deck officers

**Night Vision Test:**
- Adaptometer test
- Required for bridge watchkeeping

### 2. Hearing Tests

**Pure Tone Audiometry:**
- Testing at different frequencies
- Both ears tested separately
- Max acceptable loss: 30 dB at 500-3000 Hz

**Speech Discrimination:**
- Understanding spoken words
- Conversation at 2 meters distance

### 3. Physical Examination

**General Assessment:**
- Height and weight measurement
- BMI calculation
- Blood pressure (3 readings)
- Pulse rate
- Respiratory rate

**Systemic Examination:**
- Cardiovascular system
- Respiratory system
- Abdominal examination
- Neurological assessment
- Musculoskeletal system

### 4. Laboratory Tests

| Test | Purpose |
|------|---------|
| Complete Blood Count | General health, anemia, infections |
| Blood Sugar (Fasting) | Diabetes screening |
| Urine Analysis | Kidney function, diabetes, infections |
| Liver Function Test | Liver health |
| Kidney Function Test | Kidney health |
| HIV Test | Mandatory screening |
| HBsAg | Hepatitis B screening |
| Drug Screening | Substance abuse check |

### 5. Special Tests

**Chest X-Ray:**
- TB screening
- Lung conditions
- Heart size assessment

**ECG (Electrocardiogram):**
- Heart rhythm
- Required for candidates above 40

**Spirometry:**
- Lung function
- Especially for engine room personnel

## DG Shipping Approved Medical Examiners

Pre-sea medical must be conducted by DG Shipping approved medical examiners (AME) only.

### How to Find Approved Medical Examiners

1. Visit DG Shipping website
2. Go to "Approved Medical Examiners" section
3. Filter by city/state
4. Note the AME registration number

## Pre-Sea Medical Process

### Step 1: Get Referral (if applicable)

Some pre-sea institutes provide referral letters to specific AMEs. Check with your institute first.

### Step 2: Book Appointment

- Call AME office directly
- Book appointment in advance
- Morning appointments preferred (fasting tests)

### Step 3: Documents to Carry

- Passport
- 10th/12th certificates
- Passport-size photographs (4)
- Admission letter (if joining pre-sea course)
- Previous medical records (if any)
- Spectacle prescription (if using glasses)

### Step 4: Preparation Before Medical

**Night Before:**
- Get 7-8 hours sleep
- Avoid alcohol completely
- Avoid heavy meals
- Stay hydrated

**Morning of Medical:**
- Come fasting (8-12 hours)
- Bring water bottle
- Wear comfortable clothes
- Carry glasses/contact lenses if used
- Bring all regular medications

### Step 5: Medical Examination Day

**Duration:** 3-5 hours (depending on crowd)

**Sequence:**
1. Registration and documentation
2. Vision tests
3. Blood and urine samples
4. Physical examination
5. X-Ray and ECG
6. Final assessment by AME

### Step 6: Collect Certificate

- Same day or next day
- Certificate valid for 2 years
- Carry original during pre-sea course admission

## Tips to Pass Pre-Sea Medical

### Vision Preparation

1. **Rest your eyes** - Avoid screens 24 hours before
2. **Get enough sleep** - Fatigue affects vision
3. **Carry correct prescription glasses** - If you use them
4. **Practice color plates** - Familiarize yourself with Ishihara

### Blood Pressure Control

1. **Avoid salt** - 3 days before medical
2. **No caffeine** - Morning of medical
3. **Deep breathing** - Before BP measurement
4. **Stay calm** - "White coat hypertension" is real

### Blood Sugar Management

1. **Proper fasting** - 10-12 hours
2. **No sweets** - 3 days before
3. **Stay hydrated** - Water is okay during fasting

### Physical Fitness

1. **Regular exercise** - At least 2 months before
2. **Maintain healthy weight** - BMI 20-25 ideal
3. **Quit smoking** - At least 2 weeks before
4. **No alcohol** - At least 1 week before

## What If You Fail Pre-Sea Medical?

### Temporary Deferment

If issue is correctable (like high BP due to anxiety):
- Get treatment
- Wait 2-4 weeks
- Reappear for medical
- May need letter from specialist

### Permanent Unfitness

Some conditions permanently disqualify:
- Get proper documentation
- Consider shore-based maritime careers
- Explore related industries

### Challenging the Decision

If you believe assessment was wrong:
1. Request reassessment by different AME
2. Get specialist opinion
3. Submit appeal to DG Shipping

## Medical Requirements by Rank

### Deck Officers

- Strictest vision requirements
- Color vision mandatory
- Night vision critical
- No compromise on eyesight

### Engine Officers

- Slightly relaxed vision standards
- Color vision for safety signals
- Hearing more critical (machinery noise)

### Ratings

- General fitness required
- Vision standards relaxed
- Physical strength important

## Renewal of Medical Certificate

Medical certificates are valid for 2 years (1 year if over 50).

**Renewal Process:**
1. Book appointment with any AME
2. Undergo full medical examination
3. Same tests as initial medical
4. Certificate issued if fit

**Before Contract:**
- Companies may require medical within 3 months of joining
- Some require company-specific medical

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Can I join merchant navy with spectacles?**
A: Yes, if corrected vision meets standards. Deck officers need 6/6 corrected.

**Q: Is LASIK surgery allowed?**
A: Yes, after 6 months stabilization. Carry surgery records.

**Q: I have mild color blindness. Can I join?**
A: Engine department may be possible. Deck department requires normal color vision.

**Q: Can diabetes patients join merchant navy?**
A: Well-controlled diabetes (without insulin dependency) may be acceptable. Each case assessed individually.

**Q: What if I take regular medications?**
A: Disclose all medications. Doctor will assess if compatible with seafaring.
