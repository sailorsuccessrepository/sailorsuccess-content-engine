# RPSL Verification Guide 2026: Protect Yourself from Fake Recruitment Agencies

RPSL (Recruitment and Placement Service License) is a license issued by DG Shipping to recruitment agencies authorized to place Indian seafarers on ships. Verifying RPSL status is crucial to avoid scams and ensure you're dealing with legitimate agencies.

This guide covers everything you need to know about RPSL verification, identifying fake agencies, and protecting yourself in the maritime job market.

## What is RPSL?

RPSL stands for **Recruitment and Placement Service License**. It's a mandatory license issued by the Directorate General of Shipping (DGS) to companies that recruit and place Indian seafarers on merchant vessels.

**Key Points:**
- Issued under Merchant Shipping (Recruitment and Placement of Seafarers) Rules, 2016
- Based on MLC 2006 requirements
- Must be renewed periodically
- License displayed prominently at agency office
- Covers specific seafarer categories

**Why RPSL Matters:**
- Legal compliance for recruitment
- Seafarer protection under maritime law
- Accountability to DG Shipping
- Recourse mechanism for complaints
- Insurance requirements met

## How to Verify RPSL License

### Method 1: DG Shipping Website

**Step-by-Step Process:**

1. Visit DG Shipping official website: www.dgshipping.gov.in

2. Navigate to: Services → Seafarers → RPSL

3. Click on "List of RPSL Holders"

4. Search for the agency by:
   - Company name
   - RPSL number
   - City/Location

5. Verify details:
   - License validity date
   - Authorized categories
   - Office address
   - Contact information

### Method 2: Direct Verification Request

Contact DG Shipping seafarer section:
- Email: seafarer.dgs@gov.in
- Write letter with agency details
- Request verification

### Method 3: Physical Verification

Visit the agency office and check:
- Original RPSL license displayed
- License number matches online records
- Validity date is current
- Address matches official records

## What to Check in RPSL

**Essential Verification Points:**

| Detail | What to Verify |
|--------|----------------|
| RPSL Number | Matches DGS records |
| Company Name | Exact match with license |
| Validity Date | License is current |
| Address | Office location matches |
| Categories | Licensed for your rank |
| Principal Companies | Ships you'll be placed on |

**Understanding RPSL Categories:**

RPSL licenses specify which seafarer categories the agency can recruit:
- Officers (Deck)
- Officers (Engine)
- Ratings (Deck)
- Ratings (Engine)
- Catering staff
- All categories

Ensure the agency is licensed for YOUR rank category.

## Red Flags: Identifying Fake Agencies

**Warning Signs:**

| Red Flag | What It Means |
|----------|---------------|
| No RPSL displayed | Possibly unlicensed |
| Expired license | Illegal operation |
| Different address | Could be fake agency |
| Demands upfront payment | Potential scam |
| No proper office | Fly-by-night operation |
| Guarantees job | False promises |
| Pressure tactics | Desperate for your money |

**Common Scam Tactics:**

1. **Advance Fee Fraud:**
   - Demanding large "processing fees"
   - Asking for medical test payment to their doctor
   - Requesting "uniform deposits"
   - Charging for "file work"

2. **Fake Job Offers:**
   - Offering unrealistic salaries
   - Jobs on non-existent ships
   - Companies that don't exist
   - Immediate joining without verification

3. **Document Fraud:**
   - Collecting original documents
   - Photocopies for "processing"
   - Creating fake CDC entries

4. **Identity Theft:**
   - Using your details for other purposes
   - Creating fake documentation

## Legitimate vs. Illegitimate Agencies

**Legitimate Agency Characteristics:**

✅ RPSL prominently displayed
✅ Proper office infrastructure
✅ Clear fee structure (minimal or no fees)
✅ Transparent process
✅ Verifiable company associations
✅ Professional communication
✅ Written agreements
✅ Receipt for any payments

**Illegitimate Agency Characteristics:**

❌ No RPSL or expired license
❌ Operates from home/temporary location
❌ Demands large upfront payments
❌ Verbal promises only
❌ Pressure to decide quickly
❌ No documentation provided
❌ Cash-only transactions
❌ Guarantees unrealistic outcomes

## Steps Before Engaging Any Agency

**Due Diligence Checklist:**

1. **Verify RPSL Online**
   - Check DGS website
   - Note license number and validity

2. **Research the Agency**
   - Search online reviews
   - Check maritime forums
   - Ask other seafarers
   - Look for complaints

3. **Visit Physically**
   - See the office
   - Meet staff
   - Check displayed license
   - Assess professionalism

4. **Verify Principal Companies**
   - Check companies they claim to represent
   - Cross-verify with company websites
   - Contact companies if possible

5. **Understand Fee Structure**
   - Get everything in writing
   - Know what you're paying for
   - Keep receipts

6. **Don't Submit Originals**
   - Only certified copies initially
   - Originals only after selection
   - Keep inventory of documents submitted

## What to Do If Scammed

**Immediate Steps:**

1. **Document Everything**
   - Save all communications
   - Keep receipts
   - Note names and contacts
   - Screenshot websites

2. **File Complaint with DG Shipping**
   - Write to seafarer.dgs@gov.in
   - Describe incident in detail
   - Attach evidence
   - Provide agency details

3. **Police Complaint**
   - File FIR at local police station
   - Cybercrime cell if online fraud
   - Provide all documentation

4. **Warn Others**
   - Post on maritime forums
   - Share on social media (carefully)
   - Inform maritime institutes
   - Alert other seafarers

**DG Shipping Complaint Channels:**

- Email: dgsinfo@nic.in
- Toll-free: 1800-345-345 (if available)
- Written complaint to DGS office
- Online grievance portal

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Can agencies charge fees for placement?**
No, legitimate agencies should not charge significant fees. Per MLC 2006, the cost of recruitment should be borne by shipowners.

**What if an agency's RPSL is expired?**
Don't engage with them. Report to DG Shipping. They're operating illegally.

**How often is RPSL renewed?**
Typically every 3-5 years. Always check current validity.

**Can I verify RPSL over phone?**
Contact DG Shipping, but always verify through official website too.

**What if agency is not on DGS list?**
They're not licensed. Stay away regardless of claims.
