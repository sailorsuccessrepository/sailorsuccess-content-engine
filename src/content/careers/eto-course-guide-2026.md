# ETO Course 2026: Complete Guide

As ships become more technologically advanced, the demand for Electro-Technical Officers (ETOs) is skyrocketing. If you have an electrical or electronics background, ETO could be your fastest route to a maritime career.

## What is an ETO?

An Electro-Technical Officer is responsible for all electrical and electronic systems on board a ship, including:

- Navigation systems
- Communication equipment
- Automation and control systems
- Power generation and distribution
- Safety systems

## Course Duration

| Background | Shore Training | Sea Training |
|------------|---------------|--------------|
| Diploma | 4 months | 6 months |
| B.Tech | 4 months | 6 months |

## Course Curriculum

### Shore Phase (4 Months)

**Modules:**
- Marine electrical systems
- High voltage systems
- Automation and control
- Navigation equipment
- Communication systems
- Safety and emergency
- Workshop practice

### Sea Phase (6 Months)

**Training Record Book (TRB) tasks:**
- Electrical maintenance
- System troubleshooting
- Safety drills
- Documentation

## ETO vs Marine Engineer

| Aspect | ETO | Marine Engineer |
|--------|-----|-----------------|
| Focus | Electrical systems | Mechanical systems |
| Entry Qualification | Diploma/B.Tech EEE | B.Tech Mechanical |
| Starting Salary | $4,000 | $2,500 |
| Promotion Path | Limited | To Chief Engineer |
| Demand | High and growing | Stable |

## Advantages of ETO Career

1. **High Demand**: Modern ships need ETOs
2. **Good Starting Salary**: Higher than 4th Engineer
3. **Technical Role**: Less physical work
4. **Shorter Training**: 10 months total
5. **Growth in Automation**: Future-proof career

## Conclusion

ETO is an excellent career for electrical engineers. High demand, good pay, and technical work make it attractive. If you have the right background, consider this specialized maritime career.
