## Types of Tanker Vessels

### Oil Tankers

**Crude Oil Carriers**
- VLCC (Very Large Crude Carrier): 200,000-320,000 DWT
- Suezmax: 120,000-200,000 DWT
- Aframax: 80,000-120,000 DWT
- Panamax: 60,000-80,000 DWT

**Product Tankers**
- Carry refined petroleum products
- 25,000-80,000 DWT typically
- Multiple cargo grades per voyage
- More complex cargo operations

### Chemical Tankers

**IMO Type Classifications**
- IMO Type 1: Most hazardous cargoes
- IMO Type 2: Significant hazard cargoes
- IMO Type 3: Lesser hazard products

**Cargo Complexity**
- 20-40 separate cargo tanks
- Multiple incompatible cargoes
- Sophisticated coating systems
- Stringent cleaning requirements

### Gas Carriers (LPG/LNG)

**LPG Carriers**
- Carry propane, butane, ammonia
- Pressurized or refrigerated
- Semi-refrigerated most common

**LNG Carriers**

## Requirements for Tanker Career

### Basic Qualifications

**Educational Background**
- Class 10 or 12 for ratings
- Degree/diploma for officers
- Science subjects (PCM) preferred

**COC Requirements**
- Valid Certificate of Competency
- Appropriate watchkeeping certificate
- STCW courses as per rank

### Mandatory Tanker Training

**Oil/Chemical Tanker Familiarization (1 day)**
- Basic awareness for all tanker personnel
- Cargo characteristics and hazards
- Safety equipment and procedures
- Required before joining any tanker

**Oil Tanker Cargo Operations (5 days)**
- For officers on oil tankers
- Cargo loading/discharge procedures
- Tank cleaning and gas freeing
- Inert gas system operations
- COW (Crude Oil Washing)

**Chemical Tanker Cargo Operations (5 days)**
- For officers on chemical tankers
- Cargo compatibility and segregation
- Tank coating requirements
- Specialized safety procedures
- Cargo documentation

**Liquefied Gas Tanker Operations (5 days)**
- Basic IGF Code training
- Advanced IGF Code training
- Cargo containment systems
- Emergency procedures

### Endorsement Requirements

**Oil Tanker Endorsement**
- Oil tanker familiarization course
- Oil tanker cargo operations course
- Sea service on oil tanker (deck: 3 months, engine: varies)

**Chemical Tanker Endorsement**
- Chemical tanker familiarization
- Chemical tanker cargo operations
- Sea service on chemical tanker

**Gas Tanker Endorsement**
- Advanced IGF Code training
- Specialized simulator training
- Sea service on gas carrier

## Career Progression Path

### For Deck Officers

**Year 1-3: Junior Officer Building Experience**
- Join as 3rd Officer on product tanker
- Learn cargo operations thoroughly
- Complete chemical/oil endorsement
- Build tanker sea time

**Year 4-6: Second Officer Development**
- Specialize in cargo operations
- Handle complex loading plans
- Develop vetting inspection skills
- Consider LNG transition training

**Year 7-10: Chief Officer Mastery**
- Lead cargo operations
- Manage tanker-specific safety
- Prepare for Master examination
- Build reputation with oil majors

**Year 10+: Command**
- Master on specialized tankers
- Consider shore opportunities
- Marine superintendent roles
- Vetting inspector positions

### For Engineering Officers

**Engine Cadet to 4th Engineer**

- Specialize in tanker machinery

- Overall cargo system responsibility
- Emergency response leadership
- Budget management

## Making the Tanker Transition

### From Bulk Carriers

**Requirements**
1. Complete tanker familiarization course
2. Apply to companies with mixed fleet
3. Start on product tankers (easier transition)
4. Build endorsement through sea service

**Timeline**
- Course completion: 1 week
- Job search: 1-3 months
- Sea service for endorsement: 3-6 months
- Full tanker qualification: 6-12 months

### From Container Ships

Similar process but emphasize:
- Cargo handling experience
- Safety awareness
- Willingness to learn new systems

## Frequently Asked Questions

### What is the minimum experience needed for tanker jobs?

Most companies require 6-12 months of sea service plus tanker familiarization course. Some accept fresh graduates for training positions on product tankers.

### Are tanker jobs more dangerous than other ships?

Tankers carry inherent cargo risks, but modern safety systems and strict procedures make them very safe when protocols are followed. Fatality rates are comparable to other ship types.

### Can I switch to tankers mid-career?

Yes, many officers transition to tankers for higher salaries. Complete the required courses and target companies with mixed fleets for easier transition.

### Which tanker type pays the most?

LNG carriers offer the highest salaries, followed by crude oil tankers, then chemical tankers. Gas carriers can pay 50-70% more than general cargo ships.

### How long are contracts on tankers?

Typically 4-6 months for officers, sometimes 8 months. Some companies offer shorter 3-4 month contracts on chemical tankers with frequent crew changes.
