# Deck Officer Career Path: From Cadet to Captain

The Deck Department manages navigation, cargo operations, and safety. This guide covers every step from joining as a cadet to becoming a Master (Captain).

## Deck Officer Ranks

### Rank Hierarchy

| Rank | Responsibility | Typical Age |
|------|---------------|-------------|
| Deck Cadet | Learning | 18-22 |
| Junior Officer | Support | 22-25 |
| Third Officer | Safety/Watchkeeping | 24-28 |
| Second Officer | Navigation/Charts | 27-32 |
| Chief Officer | Cargo/Deck | 30-38 |
| Master (Captain) | Command | 35+ |

## Entry Routes

### Route 1: B.Sc Nautical Science (DNS)

**Duration:** 3 years + 1 year training

**Eligibility:**
- 12th with PCM
- 60% aggregate
- 50% in PCM individually
- Age: 17-25 years

**Process:**
1. Clear IMU-CET
2. Join DG Shipping approved institute
3. Complete 3-year degree
4. 1-year onboard training
5. Become Third Officer

### Route 2: GP Rating + Upgrade

**Duration:** 6 months training + 3 years sea service

**Eligibility:**
- 10th pass
- 40% marks
- Age: 17.5-25 years

**Process:**
1. Complete GP Rating course
2. Sail as Rating (3 years)
3. Clear Bridge Watchkeeping exam
4. Become Third Officer

### Route 3: Lateral Entry

**For:**
- Navy personnel
- Coast Guard
- Fishing vessel officers

**Process:**
- Assessment of existing qualifications
- Bridge courses
- Competency certification

## Career Progression Timeline

### Deck Cadet (0-18 months)

**Role:**
- Learning navigation
- Assisting officers
- Watchkeeping under supervision
- Maintenance duties

**Certificate:** Pre-Sea Training Certificate

**Salary:** ₹25,000 - 35,000/month + allowances

**Learning Focus:**
- Bridge equipment operation
- Chart work
- Cargo operations
- Safety procedures

### Third Officer (18 months - 4 years)

**Requirements to Become:**
- 18 months sea service as cadet
- Pass COC Class IV (Mate) exam
- Valid STCW certificates

**Role:**
- Safety Officer duties
- LSA/FFA maintenance
- Navigation watchkeeping
- Publications updating

**Certificate:** Second Mate (FG) Certificate

**Salary:** ₹1.5 - 2.5 lakhs/month

### Second Officer (4-8 years)

**Requirements to Become:**
- 12 months as Third Officer
- Sometimes 18 months
- Company promotion

**Role:**
- Navigation Officer
- Chart corrections
- Bridge equipment
- Voyage planning

**Certificate:** Second Mate (FG)

**Salary:** ₹2.5 - 4 lakhs/month

### Chief Officer (8-15 years)

**Requirements to Become:**
- 18 months as Second Officer
- Pass COC Class II (Mate) exam
- Additional STCW courses

**Role:**
- Second in command
- Cargo planning
- Deck maintenance
- Crew management

**Certificate:** Chief Mate (FG)

**Salary:** ₹4 - 7 lakhs/month

### Master/Captain (15+ years)

**Requirements to Become:**
- 18 months as Chief Officer
- Pass COC Class I (Master) exam
- Additional courses
- Master's oral exam

**Role:**
- Ship command
- Overall responsibility
- Legal representative
- Final authority

**Certificate:** Master (FG)

**Salary:** ₹8 - 15+ lakhs/month

## Certificate Requirements

| Certificate | When Required |
|-------------|---------------|
| PST | Pre-sea |
| PSSR | Pre-sea |
| EFA | Pre-sea |
| FPFF | Pre-sea |
| PSCRB | Before joining |
| AFF | Before Chief Officer |
| MFA | Before Chief Officer |

### COC (Certificate of Competency)

| Certificate | For Rank |
|-------------|----------|
| Mate (FG) Class IV | Third Officer |
| Mate (FG) Class II | Chief Officer |
| Master (FG) Class I | Master |

## Exam Preparation

### Class IV (Mate FG) Exam

**Subjects:**
- Navigation
- Cargo
- Ship Stability
- Meteorology
- Ship Construction
- Signals

**Pass Requirement:** 50% each subject

**Attempts:** Unlimited

### Class II (Chief Mate FG) Exam

**Additional Subjects:**
- Advanced Navigation
- Cargo Management
- Emergency Procedures
- Maritime Law

### Class I (Master FG) Exam

**Focus:**
- Ship Management
- Commercial Operations
- Legal Aspects
- Crisis Management

**Plus:** Oral examination

## Ship Types for Deck Officers

### Container Ships

**Pros:**
- Fast voyages
- Modern vessels
- Regular schedules

**Cons:**
- Port time short
- High stress

### Tankers

**Pros:**
- Higher salary
- Technical work
- Good experience

**Cons:**
- Hazardous cargo
- Strict safety

### Bulk Carriers

**Pros:**
- Simpler cargo
- Good starting
- Many positions

**Cons:**
- Older vessels often
- Lower salaries

### LNG/LPG Carriers

**Pros:**
- Highest salaries
- Modern ships
- Prestigious

**Cons:**
- Specialized training
- Limited positions

## Tips for Success

### As a Cadet

- Learn everything possible
- Ask questions
- Build relationships
- Document experiences
- Start exam prep early

### For Promotions

- Clear exams timely
- Get diverse ship experience
- Maintain good records
- Network within industry
- Consider tanker/gas experience

### For Command

- Develop leadership skills
- Understand commercial aspects
- Build industry reputation
- Stay updated on regulations
- Maintain fitness

## Common Challenges

### Career Delays

**Causes:**
- Failed exams
- Company delays
- Lack of vacancies
- Documentation issues

**Solutions:**
- Prepare thoroughly
- Choose good companies
- Keep documents ready
- Stay flexible

### Salary Stagnation

**Causes:**
- Same rank too long
- Same company type
- Limited experience

**Solutions:**
- Clear higher exams
- Try different vessel types
- Consider foreign companies

## Conclusion

The Deck Officer career offers:

Key success factors:

- Gain varied experience

The path from Cadet to Captain typically takes 12-18 years. Plan ahead, stay focused, and the career rewards are substantial.
