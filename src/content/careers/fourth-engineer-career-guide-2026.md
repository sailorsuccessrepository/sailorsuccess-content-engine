# Fourth Engineer in Merchant Navy 2026: Complete Career Guide

The Fourth Engineer is the entry-level officer position in the engine department of merchant vessels. As the junior-most engineer officer, you'll work closely with machinery systems, gaining hands-on experience that forms the foundation of your marine engineering career.

Fourth Engineers earn competitive salaries based on vessel type and company:

## Educational Pathway to Fourth Engineer

### Route 1: GME (Graduate Marine Engineering) - 1 Year
1. Complete B.E/B.Tech in Mechanical/Marine Engineering
2. Clear IMU-CET entrance examination
3. Join DG Shipping approved GME course (1 year)
4. Complete 6 months onboard training as Engine Cadet
5. Pass MEO Class 4 written and oral examinations
6. Obtain MEO Class 4 Certificate of Competency

### Route 2: Marine Engineering Degree (4 Years)
1. Complete 10+2 with PCM (minimum 60%)
2. Clear IMU-CET examination
3. Complete 4-year B.E Marine Engineering
4. Undergo 6 months structured shipboard training
5. Clear MEO Class 4 examinations

### Route 3: ETO to Fourth Engineer
1. Complete ETO course (6 months)
2. Sail as ETO for required period
3. Appear for MEO Class 4 with additional sea time
4. Transition to Fourth Engineer role

## Core Duties and Responsibilities

### Engine Room Watchkeeping (4-8 or 0-4 watch)
- Monitor all running machinery parameters
- Take regular rounds of engine room
- Record all readings in engine room log
- Report abnormalities to Third/Second Engineer
- Respond to bridge orders promptly

### Machinery Maintenance
**Under Third Engineer's supervision:**
- Auxiliary engine maintenance
- Purifier overhauling
- Pump maintenance and troubleshooting
- Compressor servicing
- Heat exchanger cleaning

### Specific Equipment Responsibilities
Fourth Engineers typically handle:
- **Purifiers** - FO/LO/SW purifiers
- **Air compressors** - Main and emergency
- **Steering gear** - Routine checks and maintenance
- **Fresh water generator** - Operation and maintenance
- **Sewage treatment plant** - Operation and compliance

### Fuel Management
- Fuel transfer operations
- Bunker quantity calculations
- Tank sounding and documentation
- Fuel oil treatment monitoring

### Documentation
- Machinery maintenance records
- Spare parts inventory
- Oil record book entries
- Work permits and safety documentation

## Skills Required for Fourth Engineer

### Technical Skills
1. **Machinery operation** - Diesel engines, pumps, compressors
2. **Troubleshooting** - Identifying and fixing faults
3. **Maintenance procedures** - Overhauling, repairs
4. **Electrical basics** - Motor starters, basic circuits
5. **Automation systems** - PLC, alarm monitoring

### Soft Skills
1. **Attention to detail** - Noticing parameter changes
2. **Physical fitness** - Engine room work demands
3. **Communication** - Reporting to seniors clearly
4. **Team work** - Coordination with other engineers
5. **Stress management** - Handling emergency situations

## Fourth Engineer to Third Engineer Progression

### Time Required
- Minimum 12 months as Fourth Engineer
- Total sea service varies by certificate held

### Examination Requirements
- No additional examination for Third Engineer position
- Automatic progression based on sea service
- Company-specific assessments may apply

### Focus Areas for Promotion
- Master auxiliary engine maintenance
- Learn main engine systems
- Understand automation thoroughly
- Develop independent troubleshooting skills

## Challenges Faced by Fourth Engineers

### Technical Challenges
1. **Machinery breakdowns** - First-time handling of failures
2. **Hot work** - Working in high-temperature environments
3. **Confined spaces** - Tank entries and bilge work
4. **Night watches** - Alertness during 0000-0400 watch

### Personal Challenges
1. **Heat and noise** - Engine room environment
2. **Physical demands** - Manual labor components
3. **Learning curve** - Ship machinery vs shore training
4. **Hierarchy adjustment** - Working under supervision

## Tips for Success as Fourth Engineer

### First Contract Success
1. **Study ship machinery** before joining - get manuals
2. **Build relationship** with Third Engineer (your guide)
3. **Take detailed notes** during maintenance
4. **Ask questions** when unclear
5. **Never hide mistakes** - report immediately

### Long-term Career Building
1. Complete MEO Class 2 preparation during sea time
2. Learn automation and control systems
3. Pursue tanker endorsement courses
4. Understand main engine overhaul procedures
5. Develop good documentation habits

## Frequently Asked Questions

### What is the minimum age for Fourth Engineer?
After completing engineering degree and GME, typically 23-24 years when starting as Fourth Engineer.

### Is Fourth Engineer work physically demanding?
Yes, engine room work involves physical labor, heat exposure, and confined space work. Physical fitness is essential.

### How is Fourth Engineer different from ETO?
Fourth Engineer handles mechanical machinery (engines, pumps). ETO handles electrical systems and automation. Different career tracks.

### Can mechanical engineers join as Fourth Engineer?
Yes, through GME (Graduate Marine Engineering) course approved by DG Shipping.
