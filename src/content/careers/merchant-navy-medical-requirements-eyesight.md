## Understanding the Complete Framework

DG Shipping medical fitness requirements for merchant navy - eyesight standards, color blindness test, LASIK eligibility, and more.

The merchant navy operates under strict regulatory frameworks established by international maritime organizations and national authorities. Every aspect of seafaring careers—from initial training to final certifications, from employment contracts to retirement benefits—is governed by detailed regulations designed to ensure safety, competency, and fair treatment of seafarers globally.

For Indian seafarers specifically, the Directorate General of Shipping under the Ministry of Ports, Shipping and Waterways serves as the primary regulatory authority implementing international conventions including IMO (International Maritime Organization) standards, STCW Convention requirements, MLC 2006 provisions, and national maritime legislation.

### Key Regulatory Bodies

**Directorate General of Shipping (DG Shipping)**
- Implements shipping policy and maritime legislation in India
- Conducts seafarer examinations and issues Certificates of Competency
- Approves maritime training institutes and courses
- Ensures STCW Convention compliance for Indian seafarers
- Regulates seafarer employment and welfare
- Maintains seafarer registry and documentation
- Conducts flag state and port state control inspections

**Indian Maritime University (IMU)**
- Central university under Ministry of Ports, Shipping and Waterways
- Conducts IMU-CET entrance examination for maritime courses
- Offers degree programs in Nautical Science and Marine Engineering
- Maintains academic standards for maritime education
- Operates six campuses: Chennai, Mumbai, Kolkata, Visakhapatnam, Kochi, Navi Mumbai

**International Maritime Organization (IMO)**
- UN specialized agency for maritime safety and security
- Develops international maritime conventions (SOLAS, MARPOL, STCW)
- Sets global standards for ship construction, equipment, and operations
- Promotes environmental protection in shipping
- Facilitates international cooperation in maritime matters

## Detailed Eligibility and Requirements

Specific eligibility criteria vary based on the course, rank, and department you're targeting in the merchant navy. However, fundamental requirements remain consistent across pathways.

### Educational Qualifications

**For Officer-Level Courses** (Deck and Engine Departments):
- Minimum 10+2 (12th standard) from recognized board (CBSE, ICSE, State Boards)
- Physics, Chemistry, Mathematics (PCM) mandatory at 12th level
- Minimum 60% aggregate marks in PCM subjects
- English proficiency: Minimum 50% marks in English (10th or 12th)
- Single sitting passing requirement (all subjects in one attempt)
- Improvement examination results accepted if from same board

**For Rating-Level Courses**:
- Minimum 10th standard pass from recognized board
- No specific subject requirements (PCM not mandatory)
- Basic English communication skills
- Age between 18-28 years depending on course

**For Graduate Entry Courses**:
- B.Tech Mechanical Engineering degree for GME (Graduate Marine Engineering)
- B.Tech/Diploma Electrical Engineering for ETO (Electro-Technical Officer)
- Recognized university/board certification
- Specific percentage requirements vary by institute

### Age Criteria

**Standard Age Limits**:
- Minimum age: 17 years (at time of course commencement)
- Maximum age: 25 years for most officer-level courses
- Rating courses: 18-28 years
- Graduate entry: Up to 28 years

**Age Relaxation**:
- Sponsored candidates: Extended upper limit to 28 years
- SC/ST categories: As per government reservation policies
- PWD candidates: Specific provisions under disability acts

Age is calculated as on the last date of application submission or course commencement date, depending on institute/examination rules.

### Medical Fitness Standards

DG Shipping enforces comprehensive medical fitness requirements ensuring seafarers can withstand the physically and mentally demanding nature of life at sea.

**Vision Requirements (Deck Department)**:
- Unaided distant vision: 6/6 in both eyes
- No corrective lenses permitted (glasses/contact lenses not allowed)
- Near vision: N/6 in better eye, N/9 in worse eye without glasses
- Color vision: Must pass Ishihara test for color blindness
- Night vision: Adequate adaptation to darkness
- Field of vision: Normal peripheral vision without restrictions

**Vision Requirements (Engine Department)**:
- Distant vision: 6/12 or better with or without corrective glasses
- Corrective power limit: +/- 2.5 Diopters
- Near vision: N/6 with or without correction
- Color vision: Must distinguish primary colors accurately
- No progressive degenerative eye conditions

**LASIK/Refractive Surgery**:
- Acceptable for Engine department candidates
- Must be performed at least 6 months before medical examination
- Complete healing and stability documented
- Ophthalmologist certificate confirming no complications
- Post-surgery vision must meet standard requirements
- Some deck positions may accept LASIK with specific conditions

**General Health Parameters**:
- Height and weight: Proportionate BMI (Body Mass Index 18-25 ideal)
- Blood pressure: Within normal range (120/80 ± 20 mmHg)
- Heart: No congenital defects, murmurs, or rhythm irregularities
- Lungs: Normal respiratory function, no chronic conditions
- Hearing: Pass pure tone audiometry test (500-3000 Hz range)
- Teeth: Good dental health (major dental work completed before joining)
- Spine: No deformities affecting mobility or load-bearing capacity

**Conditions Leading to Medical Rejection**:
- Diabetes mellitus (Type 1 or uncontrolled Type 2)
- Epilepsy or seizure disorders
- Cardiovascular diseases (coronary artery disease, heart failure)
- Chronic kidney or liver diseases
- Infectious diseases: HIV positive, Hepatitis B/C, active TB
- Severe psychiatric disorders
- Drug or alcohol dependency
- Major physical disabilities affecting seafaring duties
- Pregnancy during initial training (can join post-delivery with clearance)

**Medical Examination Process**:
1. Get appointment from DG Shipping approved medical examiner
2. Carry required documents: age proof, educational certificates, photographs
3. Undergo comprehensive medical tests
4. Receive medical fitness certificate if cleared
5. Certificate validity: 2 years from date of issue
6. Re-examination required if validity expires

## Step-by-Step Application Process

### Phase 1: Research and Preparation

**1. Career Decision Making** (2-3 months before application)
- Understand difference between Deck and Engine departments
- Research course options: DNS, B.Sc, B.Tech, GP Rating, etc.
- Evaluate personal interests, aptitudes, and capabilities
- Assess financial resources for course fees and expenses
- Consult with current seafarers or maritime professionals

**2. Eligibility Verification** (1 month before)
- Confirm educational qualification meets requirements
- Check age criteria and calculate age as on cut-off date
- Preliminary vision self-check (read small text, identify colors)
- Review medical history for potential disqualifying conditions
- Verify document availability (mark sheets, certificates, IDs)

**3. Institute Selection** (During application period)
- Check DG Shipping website for approved institutes list
- Compare placement records of different maritime colleges
- Evaluate fee structures and scholarship availability
- Consider location, infrastructure, and faculty credentials
- Verify IMU affiliation for degree courses
- Read student reviews and alumni feedback

### Phase 2: Entrance Examination

**IMU-CET Application** (March-April 2026)
- Visit official IMU website: www.imu.ac.in
- Register online creating applicant profile
- Fill application form with accurate personal/academic details
- Upload documents: photograph, signature, certificates (scanned copies)
- Select exam center preference (20+ cities across India)
- Pay application fee: ₹1,000 (General), ₹750 (SC/ST) via online mode
- Download admit card after release (typically 2 weeks before exam)
- Verify details on admit card for any corrections

**IMU-CET Preparation** (6 months prior)
- Study Physics, Chemistry, Mathematics at JEE Main standard
- Focus on NCERT textbooks (11th and 12th CBSE syllabus)
- Practice English comprehension and maritime aptitude
- Solve previous 5 years' IMU-CET question papers
- Take full-length mock tests simulating exam conditions
- Identify weak areas and revise systematically
- Join online test series or coaching if needed
- Maintain consistent daily study schedule

**Examination Day** (May 2026)
- Reach exam center 30 minutes before reporting time
- Carry admit card and original photo ID proof
- No electronic devices allowed (mobile phones, calculators, smartwatches)
- 3-hour computer-based test with 200 MCQs
- Answer strategy: Attempt easy questions first, manage time wisely
- Marking scheme: +4 correct, -1 incorrect (avoid wild guessing)

**Result and Counseling** (June-July 2026)
- Results declared on IMU website with rank
- Download rank card and score card
- Register for counseling process
- Prepare choice filling strategy based on rank
- Attend counseling as per scheduled date
- Document verification: original certificates mandatory
- Course and campus allotment based on rank and preferences
- Pay seat acceptance fee to confirm admission

### Phase 3: Admission and Enrollment

**Medical Examination** (After seat allotment)
- Locate DG Shipping approved medical examiner in your city
- Book appointment and visit with required documents
- Complete all medical tests prescribed
- Collect medical fitness certificate
- Validity: 2 years from issue date
- Keep original safe and carry photocopy for admission

**Company Sponsorship** (Parallel process - January to May)
- Research shipping companies offering pre-sea sponsorships
- Companies: Maersk, MSC, Anglo-Eastern, V.Ships, Bernhard Schulte
- Apply through company websites or campus recruitment
- Attend written test: Basic PCM, English, maritime aptitude
- Clear group discussion and personal interview rounds
- Undergo company-specific medical examination
- Sign training agreement upon selection
- Sponsorship covers course fees and may include stipend

**Institute Admission** (July-August 2026)
- Report to allotted maritime institute on scheduled date
- Document submission: Originals and photocopies
  - 10th mark sheet and certificate
  - 12th mark sheet and certificate
  - Transfer certificate/school leaving certificate
  - Character certificate from school principal
  - Birth certificate or age proof
  - Caste certificate (if applicable for reservation)
  - Medical fitness certificate
  - Passport size photographs (20 copies white background)
  - Aadhar card copy
  - Passport (if available, mandatory before sea training)
- Fee payment: As per institute schedule (₹3-8 lakh depending on course)
- Hostel allotment and accommodation settlement
- Uniform measurement and issue
- Library card and ID card issuance
- Attendance at orientation program

### Phase 4: Training Completion

**Pre-Sea Academic Training**
- Duration varies: 1 year (DNS), 3 years (B.Sc), 4 years (B.Tech)
- Attend theory lectures, practical workshops, lab sessions
- Simulator training: RADAR, ECDIS, Engine Room simulators
- Regular internal assessments and semester examinations
- Maintain 75% minimum attendance (as per IMU norms)
- Complete project work and assignments
- Participate in ship visits and industrial training programs
- Clear all papers to be eligible for onboard training

**STCW Courses** (Mandatory before sea joining)
- PST (Personal Safety and Social Responsibilities): 5 days
- PSSR (Personal Survival and Social Responsibilities): 5 days
- EFA (Elementary First Aid): 3 days
- PFFP (Fire Prevention and Fire Fighting): 5 days
- Total duration: 3-4 weeks
- Practical hands-on training with firefighting, life-saving equipment
- Written and practical assessments
- Certificates issued valid for 5 years (revalidation required)

**CDC Application** (After STCW completion)
- Apply for Continuous Discharge Certificate at DG Shipping office
- Submit application form with documents and fees
- Biometric enrollment: Photo and all 10 fingerprints
- Processing time: 15-30 days
- CDC validity: 10 years
- Essential document for signing on any ship

**Onboard Training** (Sea phase)
- DNS: 18 months onboard training as Deck Cadet
- B.Tech ME: Summer training + final semester onboard
- Join assigned vessel through company/institute placement
- Training Record Book (TRB) maintenance mandatory
- Work under supervision of senior officers
- Learn practical navigation, cargo operations, engineering
- Monthly stipend: ₹20,000-45,000 depending on company
- Free accommodation, food, uniform provided onboard

### Phase 5: Licensing and Employment

**Competency Examination Eligibility**
- Complete required sea service:
  - Deck: 18 months for OOW (Officer of the Watch)
  - Engine: Specific months for MEO Class IV
- Discharge book entries verified
- Training record book signed and stamped
- Submit application to DG Shipping for exam permission

**Examination Process**
- Written papers: Multiple subjects as per syllabus
- Practical assessments where applicable
- Oral examination: Face-to-face with DG Shipping examiners
- Passing criteria: Minimum 50% in each paper
- Results declared within 2-4 weeks
- Certificate of Competency issued upon passing

**First Job Placement**
- If sponsored: Join same company as per agreement
- If non-sponsored: Campus placements or apply to shipping companies
- Prepare professional CV highlighting training and certifications
- Attend interviews and written tests
- Sign employment contract: Review terms carefully
- Join first vessel as Third Officer or Fourth Engineer

## Career Progression and Growth

The merchant navy offers clearly defined career progression pathways with promotion based on sea time and competency examinations.

**Deck Department Progression**:
- Deck Cadet (Training period: 18 months)
- Third Officer (2-3 years experience + OOW exam)
- Second Officer (12 months as Third Officer + Second Mate exam)
- Chief Officer (12 months as Second Officer + Chief Mate exam)
- Master/Captain (18 months as Chief Officer + Master exam)

**Engine Department Progression**:
- Trainee Marine Engineer (Training period varies)
- Fourth Engineer (After Class IV MEO exam)
- Third Engineer (12 months as Fourth Engineer + Class III exam)
- Second Engineer (12 months as Third Engineer + Class II exam)
- Chief Engineer (18 months as Second Engineer + Class I exam)

**Typical Timeline to Top Rank**: 10-15 years from cadet to Captain/Chief Engineer for consistent performers

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q1: What is the success rate of merchant navy careers?**
A: Approximately 70-80% of trained seafarers successfully complete initial certification and join ships. Career retention rate varies - many continue 10-20 years, while some transition to shore jobs after 5-10 years experience.

**Q2: Can I join without sponsorship?**
A: Yes, you can self-finance your training and seek jobs after course completion. However, sponsorship is beneficial as it eliminates financial burden and guarantees employment.

**Q3: What if I fail medical examination?**
A: Depending on the issue, you may get time to rectify (dental work, weight loss, etc.) and re-appear. Vision-related rejections for Deck are usually final, but Engine department remains an option.

**Q4: Is English fluency mandatory?**
A: Yes, maritime communication globally occurs in English. You must understand and speak basic maritime English to work onboard ships and clear examinations.

**Q5: Can I work on cruise ships?**
A: Yes, cruise ship careers are available but require additional hospitality training. Salaries and working conditions differ from cargo ships.

**Q6: What is sea-sickness and how to handle it?**
A: Seasickness is nausea from ship motion. Most people adapt within days. Medications and techniques help initially; it typically disappears with exposure.

**Q7: Can I choose which countries' ships to work on?**
A: After gaining experience and required certifications, you can apply to various flag vessels. Indian CoC is internationally recognized under STCW.

**Q8: Is there age relaxation for experienced individuals?**
A: Age limits are strictly enforced for initial training courses. However, lateral entry options like GME for mechanical engineers may have different criteria.

**Q9: What happens during contract breaks?**
A: You're on leave (typically 3-4 months), receive leave salary, can spend time with family. Many pursue additional certifications or enjoy vacation before rejoining.

**Q10: Can females have successful merchant navy careers?**
A: Absolutely. Many female officers serve successfully as Deck and Engine officers. Industry is increasingly gender-inclusive with proper facilities and safety measures.
