# Shipping Company Recruitment Process 2026: Complete Guide

Getting hired by a reputable shipping company is crucial for a successful maritime career. This guide covers the complete recruitment process, from application to onboarding.

## Types of Hiring Companies

### Ship Owners

| Type | Description | Examples |
|------|-------------|----------|
| National | Government-owned | SCI (Shipping Corporation of India) |
| Private Indian | Indian-owned | Great Eastern, Essar |
| Foreign | International owners | Maersk, MSC, CMA CGM |

### Ship Managers

| Company Type | Role | Examples |
|--------------|------|----------|
| Third-party managers | Manage ships for owners | Anglo-Eastern, BSM, Fleet Management |
| Technical managers | Technical operations | V.Ships, Synergy |
| Crewing agencies | Recruit seafarers | Various agencies |

### Manning Agencies

| Type | Function | Notes |
|------|----------|-------|
| RPSL agencies | DG Shipping approved | Recruit for multiple companies |
| Company-direct | Internal recruitment | Own fleet staffing |
| Training-tied | Institute partnerships | Sponsored training |

## Recruitment Timeline

|-------|----------|----------|

| Shortlisting | 1-2 weeks after application | Variable |

### Experienced Seafarer Recruitment

| Stage | Timeline | Duration |
|-------|----------|----------|

| Document verification | 1-2 weeks | Quick |
| Interview (if required) | 1 week | Brief |
| Medical | Before joining | 1-2 days |
| Joining | As per vacancy | Variable |

## Medical Requirements

### Pre-Employment Medical Examination (PEME)

**Tests Conducted:**

| Test | Purpose |
|------|---------|
| Physical examination | Overall fitness |
| Blood tests | Health markers, drug screening |
| Urine test | Drug detection, medical conditions |
| Chest X-ray | Respiratory health |
| ECG | Heart function |
| Vision test | Eyesight standards |
| Hearing test | Auditory fitness |

**Medical Standards:**

| Parameter | Deck | Engine |
|-----------|------|--------|
| Visual acuity | 6/6 essential | 6/9 minimum |
| Color vision | Normal required | Normal preferred |
| Height | Min 157 cm | Min 157 cm |
| BMI | 18-30 | 18-30 |
| Blood pressure | Normal range | Normal range |

**Common Disqualifying Conditions:**

| Condition | Impact |
|-----------|--------|
| Color blindness | Disqualified (deck) |
| Diabetes (uncontrolled) | May disqualify |
| Heart conditions | Case-by-case |
| Psychiatric disorders | Usually disqualified |
| HIV positive | Company-dependent |

## Tips for Successful Recruitment

### Before Application

1. **Research thoroughly** - Know the company
2. **Update documents** - All certificates valid
3. **Professional resume** - Clear, complete
4. **Network** - Connect with company employees

### During Interview

1. **Dress professionally** - First impression matters
2. **Be punctual** - Arrive 30 minutes early
3. **Prepare technically** - Revise fundamentals
4. **Show enthusiasm** - Express genuine interest
5. **Ask questions** - Show curiosity

### After Selection

1. **Complete formalities promptly** - Don't delay
2. **Maintain fitness** - Don't fail medical
3. **Keep documents ready** - Multiple copies
4. **Stay in touch** - With HR/manning

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

| Mistake | Consequence |
|---------|-------------|
| Applying without research | Poor interview performance |
| Incomplete documents | Delayed processing |
| Overconfidence | Negative impression |
| Salary negotiation (first job) | May lose opportunity |
| Ignoring medical fitness | Failed PEME |

## Frequently Asked Questions

### How long does recruitment take?

Typically 4-8 weeks from application to joining.

### Can I apply to multiple companies?

Yes, but inform companies if you accept another offer.

### What if I fail the medical?

Treat the condition and reapply after recovery.

### Is experience always required?

Fresh graduates are hired for trainee/cadet positions.

### How important are references?

Very important for experienced seafarers, less critical for freshers.

## Conclusion

Shipping company recruitment requires preparation, persistence, and professionalism. Research companies thoroughly, prepare your documents well, and present yourself confidently in interviews.

Choose companies based on career growth potential, not just immediate salary. Your first company shapes your entire maritime career trajectory.
