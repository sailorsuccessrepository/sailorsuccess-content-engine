# Anglo-Eastern Ship Management: Complete Hiring Guide 2026

Anglo-Eastern is one of the world's largest independent ship management companies, managing over 650 vessels globally. For Indian seafarers, it represents one of the best career opportunities in the maritime industry. This guide covers everything you need to know about joining Anglo-Eastern.

## About Anglo-Eastern Ship Management

Founded in 1974 and headquartered in Hong Kong, Anglo-Eastern has grown to become a maritime giant with operations spanning the globe.

### Company Overview

| Aspect | Details |
|--------|---------|
| Founded | 1974 |
| Headquarters | Hong Kong |
| Fleet Size | 650+ vessels |
| Employees | 30,000+ seafarers |
| Vessel Types | Tankers, Bulkers, Containers, LNG, Offshore |
| India Office | Mumbai, Chennai |

### Why Seafarers Prefer Anglo-Eastern

Anglo-Eastern consistently ranks among the top employers for Indian seafarers due to several factors. They offer competitive salaries that often exceed industry averages by 10-15%. The company invests heavily in crew welfare programs including mental health support, family benefits, and recreational facilities onboard.

Career progression at Anglo-Eastern is merit-based with clear pathways from Junior Officer to Master or Chief Engineer. Their training centers in Mumbai and Manila provide world-class simulation facilities and competency development programs.

## Available Positions

### Deck Department

Anglo-Eastern recruits for all deck positions from Deck Cadet to Master.

**Deck Cadet Requirements:**
- B.Sc. Nautical Science or equivalent
- Valid CDC and INDOS
- Age below 25 years
- Clear medical fitness

**Junior Officer (3rd/2nd Officer) Requirements:**
- Valid COC
- Minimum 6 months sea service as cadet
- GMDSS certificate
- All STCW certificates

**Senior Officer (Chief Officer/Master) Requirements:**
- Valid higher COC
- Minimum experience as per DG Shipping norms
- Company's internal assessment clearance

### Engine Department

Engineering positions from Engine Cadet to Chief Engineer are available.

**Engine Cadet Requirements:**
- B.E./B.Tech Marine Engineering or GME
- Valid CDC and INDOS
- Age below 25 years
- Clear medical fitness

**Junior Engineer (4th/3rd Engineer) Requirements:**
- Valid MEO Class 4 COC
- Minimum sea service as per regulations
- All STCW certificates

**Senior Engineer (2nd Engineer/Chief Engineer) Requirements:**
- Valid MEO Class 1 or 2 COC
- Substantial experience in relevant rank
- Company assessment clearance

### Ratings

Anglo-Eastern also recruits ratings across departments.

**Positions Available:**
- Able Seaman (AB)
- Ordinary Seaman (OS)
- Bosun
- Motorman/Oiler
- Fitter
- Pump Man
- Electrician

## Application Process

### Step 1: Online Application

Visit the Anglo-Eastern careers portal at their official website. Create a profile and upload your documents including CDC, COC, STCW certificates, educational certificates, and sea service documents. Ensure all documents are clear scans with valid dates.

### Step 2: Document Verification

Anglo-Eastern's recruitment team verifies all submitted documents. This typically takes 7-14 days. They may request additional documents or clarifications during this phase.

### Step 3: Written Test

Shortlisted candidates receive invitations for written tests. Tests are conducted at Mumbai and Chennai offices.

**Test Pattern:**
- Technical questions (60%)
- General aptitude (20%)
- English proficiency (20%)
- Duration: 2 hours

### Step 4: Simulator Assessment

Candidates clearing written tests undergo simulator assessments at Anglo-Eastern Maritime Academy.

**Assessment Areas:**
- Bridge/Engine room operations
- Emergency response
- Communication skills
- Decision-making under pressure

### Step 5: Interview

Final interviews are conducted by senior captains or chief engineers along with HR representatives.

**Interview Focus:**
- Technical competency
- Safety awareness
- Company culture fit
- Career aspirations

### Step 6: Medical Examination

Selected candidates undergo medical examination at DG Shipping approved centers. Anglo-Eastern has strict medical standards given their diverse fleet operations.

### Step 7: Pre-joining Training

Before first assignment, candidates complete company-specific training covering vessel familiarization, company policies, and safety procedures.

## Fleet Types and Opportunities

Anglo-Eastern manages diverse vessel types offering varied career opportunities.

### Tankers

Their tanker fleet includes crude oil carriers, product tankers, and chemical tankers. Tanker operations offer higher salaries but require additional STCW endorsements like Oil Tanker Familiarization and Specialized Training.

### LNG/LPG Carriers

Gas carriers represent the premium segment with highest salaries. Anglo-Eastern's LNG fleet serves major energy companies globally. Getting into gas carriers typically requires 2-3 years experience on conventional vessels first.

### Bulk Carriers

The bulk carrier fleet handles grain, coal, iron ore, and other dry commodities. These vessels offer good entry points for freshers with opportunities to progress to specialized vessels later.

### Container Vessels

Container operations involve fast turnaround times and frequent port calls. These vessels suit seafarers who prefer varied port experiences and faster pace.

### Offshore Support

Anglo-Eastern also manages offshore support vessels serving oil and gas platforms. These operations involve unique challenges and attractive compensation.

## Training and Development

Anglo-Eastern invests significantly in seafarer development through their maritime academies and training programs.

### Anglo-Eastern Maritime Academy

Located in Mumbai, the academy offers state-of-the-art simulation facilities for bridge operations, engine room operations, cargo handling, and crisis management. Training is mandatory for all joining officers and periodic refreshers are scheduled during leave periods.

### Competency Development Program

The company runs structured competency development ensuring officers meet both regulatory requirements and company standards. This includes technical assessments, soft skills training, and leadership development for senior officers.

### Sponsorship Programs

Anglo-Eastern sponsors promising cadets through maritime education. Sponsored cadets receive full course fees, monthly stipends, guaranteed employment post-training, and accelerated career progression.

## Career Progression Path

### Deck Department Progression

The typical career path spans from Deck Cadet through 3rd Officer, 2nd Officer, Chief Officer, to Master over 12-15 years with consistent performance and timely certifications.

### Engine Department Progression

Engineers progress from Engine Cadet through 4th Engineer, 3rd Engineer, 2nd Engineer, to Chief Engineer. This progression typically takes 12-15 years with proper certifications and assessments.

### Shore-Based Opportunities

Anglo-Eastern also offers shore-based career paths for experienced seafarers including Fleet Superintendent, Marine Superintendent, Technical Superintendent, Training Instructor, and Recruitment and Crewing Manager positions.

## Interview Preparation Tips

### Technical Preparation

Review your fundamentals thoroughly covering SOLAS, MARPOL, ISM Code, ISPS Code, and vessel-specific operations. Be prepared to discuss real scenarios from your sea service with clear explanation of your role and decisions.

### Company Research

Understand Anglo-Eastern's history, fleet composition, recent news, safety record, and company values. Interviewers appreciate candidates who demonstrate genuine interest in the company.

### Document Preparation

Organize all documents chronologically and ensure validity dates are current. Carry original documents along with clear photocopies. Keep a summary sheet of your sea service ready.

### Communication Skills

Practice articulating your experiences clearly. Use the STAR method (Situation, Task, Action, Result) for behavioral questions. Maintain professional demeanor throughout the interview process.

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

### During Application

Avoid submitting incomplete applications or documents with expired validity. Don't exaggerate experience or qualifications as verification is thorough. Ensure contact information is accurate and monitored.

### During Interview

Don't speak negatively about previous employers or colleagues. Avoid overconfidence or underconfidence in technical discussions. Don't negotiate salary aggressively during initial interviews.

### Post Selection

Don't delay document updates or medical examination. Respond promptly to all communications. Complete pre-joining formalities within specified timelines.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### How long does the hiring process take?
The complete process from application to joining typically takes 2-4 months depending on position and document readiness.

### Does Anglo-Eastern hire freshers?
Yes, they have cadet programs and recruit freshers for junior positions across departments.

### What is the contract duration?
Standard contracts range from 4-9 months depending on vessel type and rank.

### Is there a joining bond?
Sponsored cadets have bond obligations. Direct recruits typically don't have bonds.

### How often are promotions available?
Promotions depend on performance assessments, sea service completion, and COC upgrades. Typical promotion cycles are 2-3 years per rank.
