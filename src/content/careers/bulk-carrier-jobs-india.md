# Bulk Carrier Jobs India 2026: Complete Guide

Bulk carriers are the workhorses of global shipping, carrying dry bulk cargo like coal, iron ore, grain, and fertilizers. For Indian seafarers, bulk carriers represent one of the most accessible entry points into the merchant navy.

## What is a Bulk Carrier?

A bulk carrier (also called a bulker) is a merchant ship designed to transport unpackaged bulk commodities. These ships carry:

- **Dry bulk**: Coal, iron ore, grain, bauxite, fertilizers
- - **Minor bulk**: Steel, cement, sugar, salt
  - - **Agribulk**: Wheat, corn, soybeans
   
    - ### Types of Bulk Carriers by Size

    - | Handysize | 15,000–35,000 | Regional, coastal |

    - | Panamax | 65,000–90,000 | Pacific, Atlantic |
    - | Capesize | 100,000–200,000+ | Brazil-China, Australia-China |
   
    - ## Why Choose Bulk Carriers?
   
    - ### Advantages for Indian Seafarers
   
    - **1. Easier Entry**
    - Bulk carriers are considered easier to work on compared to tankers or container ships. Less complex cargo operations make them ideal for freshers.
   
    - **2. Strong Indian Presence**
    - Many Indian shipping companies operate bulk carriers, making it easier to get your first contract.
   
    - **3. Good Salary**
    - Bulk carrier salaries are competitive and often better than general cargo ships.
   
    - **4. Regular Schedules**
    - Bulk carriers typically have more predictable schedules compared to container ships.

                        - ## How to Get Bulk Carrier Jobs as an Indian Seafarer
                       
                        - ### Step 1: Complete Required Qualifications
                        - - DNS (Diploma in Nautical Science) for deck officers
                          - - GME (Graduate Marine Engineering) for engineers
                            - - GP Rating course for ratings
                             
                              - ### Step 2: Obtain Required Certificates
                              - - STCW Basic Safety Training
                                - - PDSD (Proficiency in Designated Security Duties)
                                  - - Medical fitness certificate (ENG1 or equivalent)
                                    - - INDOS number registration
