## Maersk Fleet and Operations

### Container Division

Maersk Line operates one of the world's largest container fleets. Vessels range from feeder ships to ultra-large container vessels exceeding 20,000 TEU. Trade routes cover every major global shipping lane.

### Maersk Tankers

The tanker division manages crude oil, product, and chemical tankers. Though now a separate entity (Hafnia), Maersk continues tanker operations through partnerships.

### Maersk Supply Service

Offshore support vessels serving oil and gas installations. These operations continue though strategic reviews are ongoing.

### Svitzer

Tugboat and maritime services operations in major ports worldwide. Svitzer offers unique career opportunities in harbor operations.

## Available Positions

### Deck Department

Maersk recruits deck officers at all levels with emphasis on quality and potential.

**Deck Cadet Program**
Maersk's prestigious cadet program offers structured training, guaranteed employment post-training, and accelerated career development.

Requirements include B.Sc. Nautical Science or equivalent, age below 24, strong academic record, English proficiency, and leadership potential.

**Deck Officers**
3rd Officer, 2nd Officer, and Chief Officer positions are filled through direct recruitment and internal promotion.

Requirements include valid COC, relevant sea service, container ship experience (preferred), and strong safety record.

**Master**
Command positions are typically filled through internal progression. External Masters with exceptional records may be considered for specific requirements.

### Engine Department

Engineering positions from cadet to Chief Engineer are available.

**Engine Cadet Program**
Similar to deck program with focus on marine engineering competencies.

Requirements include GME, B.E./B.Tech Marine Engineering, age below 24, strong technical aptitude, and English proficiency.

**Engineering Officers**
4th Engineer through Chief Engineer positions recruited based on competency and experience.

Requirements include valid MEO COC, relevant sea service, modern vessel experience, and demonstrated technical competency.

### Electrical and ETO

Electro-Technical Officers are increasingly important given vessel automation.

Requirements include ETO COC, electrical engineering background, automation systems experience, and PLC and control systems knowledge.

## Maersk Recruitment Process

### Step 1: Online Application

Apply through Maersk's careers portal. Create a comprehensive profile with all maritime credentials, sea service records, and certifications.

### Step 2: Initial Screening

HR reviews applications for basic eligibility. Automated screening may filter based on minimum requirements.

### Step 3: Assessment Center

Shortlisted candidates attend assessment centers including technical tests covering relevant competencies, psychometric evaluation, group exercises demonstrating teamwork, and individual interviews.

### Step 4: Final Interview

Panel interview with senior maritime officers. Focus on safety awareness, problem-solving, leadership potential, and cultural fit.

### Step 5: Pre-Employment Checks

Background verification, reference checks, and document authentication.

### Step 6: Medical Examination

Comprehensive medical at approved centers. Maersk maintains high medical standards.

### Step 7: Pre-Joining Training

New joiners complete company-specific training at Maersk Training Centre Mumbai covering safety procedures, company policies, fleet familiarization, and simulator assessments.

## Maersk Cadet Program

### Program Structure

The Maersk cadet program is among the industry's most comprehensive. Cadets receive structured training plan throughout pre-sea course, guaranteed sea time on modern vessels, mentorship from experienced officers, accelerated promotion upon completion, and competitive stipends during training.

### Selection Process

Cadet selection is highly competitive. Maersk looks for academic excellence in relevant subjects, leadership demonstrated through extracurriculars, communication skills in English, problem-solving ability, and long-term career commitment.

### Training Phases

Phase 1 involves pre-sea training at approved institutes with Maersk monitoring progress.

Phase 2 consists of sea training onboard Maersk vessels under officer supervision.

Phase 3 includes post-sea training and competency certification.

Phase 4 leads to employment as junior officer with structured development.

## Career Progression at Maersk

### Clear Pathways

Maersk offers transparent career progression. Each rank has defined competency requirements. Training support ensures officers meet promotion criteria.

### Typical Progression Timeline

Deck Cadet to 3rd Officer takes 2-3 years. 3rd Officer to 2nd Officer takes 2-3 years. 2nd Officer to Chief Officer takes 3-4 years. Chief Officer to Master takes 3-5 years.

Similar timelines apply for engineering ranks.

### Shore Opportunities

Experienced seafarers can transition to shore roles including Fleet Manager, Marine Superintendent, Technical Superintendent, HSEQ Manager, and Training and Development roles.
