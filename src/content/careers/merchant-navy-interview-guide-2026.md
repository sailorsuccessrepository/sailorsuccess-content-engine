# Merchant Navy Interview Questions 2026

Your interview is the final gate to your maritime career. Companies reject 70% of candidates at this stage. This guide ensures you're in the 30% who make it.

## Interview Structure

### Typical Format

| Round | Duration | Focus |
|-------|----------|-------|
| HR Round | 15-30 mins | Personality, motivation |
| Technical Round | 30-45 mins | Subject knowledge |
| Psychometric Test | 30 mins | Personality assessment |
| Medical | 2-3 hours | Physical fitness |

## HR Interview Questions

### About Yourself

**Q: Tell me about yourself.**

Good Answer Structure:
1. Name and background
2. Education
3. Why maritime
4. What you bring
5. Career goals

**Q: Why Merchant Navy?**

✅ Good answers:
- Genuine passion for sea
- Career growth opportunities
- Travel and experience
- Family maritime background

❌ Avoid:
- "Good salary"
- "Easy money"
- "Nothing else worked"

### About the Company

**Q: Why our company?**

Research and mention:
- Company's fleet
- Safety record
- Growth opportunities
- Company values

**Q: What do you know about us?**

Must know:
- Fleet size and types
- Headquarters
- Key services
- Recent news

### Personal Qualities

**Q: What are your strengths?**

Relevant strengths:
- Adaptability
- Team player
- Quick learner
- Discipline
- Stress management

**Q: What are your weaknesses?**

Smart answers:
- "I'm still improving my technical knowledge"
- "I sometimes focus too much on details"
- "I'm working on public speaking"

### Situational Questions

**Q: How do you handle stress?**

Show:
- Self-awareness
- Healthy coping mechanisms
- Previous examples

**Q: Can you work in a team?**

Give examples:
- College projects
- Sports teams
- Group activities

## Technical Interview Questions

### For Deck Cadets/Officers

**Navigation:**
- What is COLREG?
- Explain buoyage system
- What is ECDIS?
- How does GPS work?
- Explain magnetic variation

**Cargo:**
- What is GM?
- Explain stability
- Types of cargo ships
- Loading precautions
- Cargo documentation

**Safety:**
- SOLAS requirements
- Fire fighting systems
- Life-saving appliances
- Emergency procedures
- ISM Code basics

### For Engine Cadets/Officers

**Marine Engineering:**
- 4-stroke vs 2-stroke engines
- Fuel oil system
- Cooling water system
- Lubrication system
- Starting air system

**Electrical:**
- Ship's power generation
- Emergency generator
- UMS operations
- Electrical safety

**Safety:**
- Engine room fire fighting
- Enclosed space entry
- Personal protective equipment
- Risk assessment

### Common Technical Questions

**Q: What is ISM Code?**

Answer: International Safety Management Code ensures ship operators maintain safety management systems.

**Q: What is MARPOL?**

Answer: Marine Pollution Convention with 6 annexes covering oil, chemicals, sewage, garbage, air, and noxious substances.

**Q: What is STCW?**

Answer: Standards of Training, Certification, and Watchkeeping—international convention for seafarer training standards.

## Interview Day

### Morning Routine

- Wake early
- Good breakfast
- Reach 30 minutes early
- Use washroom before
- Switch phone off

### Body Language

✅ Good:
- Firm handshake
- Eye contact
- Sit straight
- Smile naturally
- Nod while listening

❌ Avoid:
- Slouching
- Fidgeting
- Looking around
- Crossing arms
- Shaking legs

### Speaking Tips

1. Clear and confident
2. Not too fast, not slow
3. Brief answers (1-2 minutes)
4. Use examples
5. Ask for clarity if needed

## After the Interview

### Follow Up

- Thank you email (same day)
- Be patient (1-2 weeks)
- Don't pester HR
- Prepare for next company

### If Not Selected

- Request feedback
- Identify gaps
- Improve and reapply
- Try other companies

## Sample Mock Interview

I've been fascinated by ships since childhood—my uncle is a Chief Officer. I believe my discipline, swimming ability, and enthusiasm make me suitable for this career. **You**: "Three reasons, sir. First, I'm academically strong with consistent performance. Second, I'm physically fit—I swim regularly and play football. Third, I'm committed to this career, not just trying it out. I've researched your company thoroughly, and your safety record and training program align with my values. I will work hard to justify your investment."
