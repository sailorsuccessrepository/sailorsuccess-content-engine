# Health Insurance for Seafarers 2026

**One medical emergency can wipe out years of savings.** Seafarers face unique health risks, and relying solely on company insurance is a mistake. This guide helps you choose the right health coverage for yourself and family.

## Why Seafarers Need Personal Health Insurance

### Company Insurance Limitations

| Factor | Reality |
|--------|---------|
| Coverage period | Only during contract |
| Gap between contracts | Often NO coverage |
| Family coverage | Usually excluded |
| Post-retirement | Nothing |
| Choice of hospital | Company decides |

**The gap problem:** If you're home for 3 months between contracts and have a health issue - you're on your own.

## Types of Health Insurance for Seafarers

### 1. P&I Club Insurance (Company Provided)

**What it covers:**
- Illness during contract
- Injury onboard
- Medical repatriation
- Death benefits

**Limitations:**
- Only while employed
- Company decides treatment
- No family coverage
- Ends with contract

### 2. Personal Health Insurance

**Benefits:**
- Continuous coverage
- Your choice of hospitals
- Family included
- Home between contracts
- Post-retirement coverage

### 3. Family Floater Plans

**Best for:**
- Self + spouse + children
- Single premium for all
- Sum insured shared
- Cost-effective

### 4. NRI-Specific Health Plans

**For NRI status seafarers:**
- Coverage in multiple countries
- Higher sum insured
- Dollar-denominated options
- Premium in INR or USD

## Coverage for Family

### While You're at Sea

**Family needs coverage for:**
- Spouse health issues
- Children emergencies
- Parents (if dependent)
- Pregnancy and delivery

### Family Floater vs Individual

| Factor | Family Floater | Individual |
|--------|----------------|------------|
| Cost | Lower overall | Higher total |
| Sum insured | Shared | Each person gets full |
| Best for | Healthy family | One member with issues |
| Flexibility | Less | More |

**Recommendation:** Family floater for young families, consider separate plan for parents above 60.

## Making Claims

### During Employment (Company Insurance)

1. Report to Master immediately
2. Get proper documentation
3. Company coordinates with P&I
4. Follow company procedures

### Personal Insurance Claims

**Cashless claim:**
1. Contact insurance helpline
2. Get pre-authorization
3. Hospital bills insurer directly
4. Pay deductibles only

**Reimbursement claim:**
1. Pay hospital bills
2. Collect all documents
3. Submit to insurer within timeframe
4. Receive reimbursement

### Documents Needed

- Policy copy
- Hospital bills (original)
- Doctor prescriptions
- Test reports
- Discharge summary
- KYC documents
- Cancelled cheque

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

1. **Relying only on company insurance**
2. **Low sum insured** - "It won't happen to me"
3. **Not covering parents** - Medical emergencies increase with age
4. **Letting policies lapse** - Between contracts
5. **Not declaring pre-existing conditions** - Claims rejected later
6. **Ignoring mental health** - Coverage often excluded
7. **Waiting too long** - Premiums increase with age
