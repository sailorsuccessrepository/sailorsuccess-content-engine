# Types of Ships in Merchant Navy: Complete Guide

Different ships mean different working conditions, salaries, and experiences. This guide covers all major vessel types to help you make informed career choices.

## Overview: Ship Categories

### By Cargo Type

| Category | Examples |
|----------|----------|
| Dry Cargo | Container, Bulk, General Cargo |
| Liquid Cargo | Tankers (Oil, Chemical, LNG) |
| Passenger | Cruise, Ferry |
| Specialized | Offshore, Research, Naval |

### By Salary Level

| Tier | Ship Types | Premium |
|------|------------|---------|
| Highest | LNG, LPG Carriers | +50-80% |
| High | Oil Tankers, Cruise | +30-50% |
| Standard | Container, Bulk | Baseline |
| Variable | Offshore | Depends on project |

## 1. Container Ships

### Overview

**Purpose:** Transport goods in standardized containers (TEUs)

**Size Range:** 1,000 - 24,000 TEU

**Major Companies:** MSC, Maersk, CMA CGM, COSCO, Hapag-Lloyd

### Working Conditions

| Factor | Rating |
|--------|--------|
| Port Time | Short (24-48 hours) |
| Cargo Work | Minimal (cranes do work) |
| Schedule | Fixed routes, tight |
| Maintenance | Moderate |
| Shore Leave | Limited |

### Pros and Cons

**Pros:**
- Modern ships
- Clean cargo
- Predictable routes
- Good companies
- Fast voyages

**Cons:**
- Very short port stays
- Time pressure
- Less shore leave
- Demanding schedules

### Salary Range

| Rank | Monthly (₹) |
|------|-------------|
| Junior Officer | 2-3 lakhs |
| Senior Officer | 4-6 lakhs |
| Master/CE | 8-12 lakhs |

## Choosing the Right Ship Type

### For Beginners

**Recommended:**
1. Container ships - Good training, modern
2. Bulk carriers - Available positions, learning
3. General cargo - Experience variety

**Avoid Initially:**
- LNG (need experience first)
- Chemical tankers (specialized)

### For Career Growth

**Best For Higher Salaries:**
1. LNG carriers
2. LPG carriers
3. Oil tankers
4. Chemical tankers

**Best For Experience:**
1. Tankers (any type)
2. Offshore
3. Cruise ships

### For Work-Life Balance

**Better Options:**
1. Cruise ships (shorter contracts, facilities)
2. Offshore (rotation schedules)
3. Tankers (longer port stays)

**Challenging:**
1. Container ships (tight schedules)
2. Bulk carriers (old ships sometimes)
