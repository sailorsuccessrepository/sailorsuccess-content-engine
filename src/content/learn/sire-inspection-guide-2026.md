# SIRE 2.0 Inspection Guide 2026

**SIRE inspections can make or break your tanker career.** A poor SIRE report affects the ship, company, and your reputation. This comprehensive guide prepares you for the new SIRE 2.0 inspection framework.

## What Changed with SIRE 2.0?

### SIRE 1.0 vs SIRE 2.0

| Aspect | SIRE 1.0 | SIRE 2.0 |
|--------|----------|----------|
| Questions | Fixed questionnaire | Dynamic, risk-based |
| Approach | Checklist-based | Competency-focused |
| Documentation | Paper-heavy | Digital-first |
| Duration | 8-12 hours | Similar, more thorough |
| Focus | Compliance | Understanding + Compliance |

### Key Features of SIRE 2.0

1. **Human Element Approach** - Focus on crew competency
2. **Risk-Based Questions** - Questions based on ship type/trade
3. **Dynamic Questionnaire** - Questions adapt during inspection
4. **Digital Platform** - Electronic reporting
5. **Continuous Improvement** - Feedback incorporated

## SIRE 2.0 Structure

### Inspection Domains

| Domain | Focus Area |
|--------|-----------|
| Management | SMS, communication, planning |
| Crew | Competency, training, wellbeing |
| Navigation | Bridge equipment, watchkeeping |
| Operations | Cargo, mooring, safety |
| Safety | Fire, LSA, drills |
| Environment | MARPOL, ballast, garbage |
| Structural | Hull, tanks, maintenance |
| Engineering | Machinery, maintenance |

### Question Categories

**Operator Questions (O):**
- Directed to specific crew members
- Tests understanding, not just knowledge
- "Show me" and "Explain" format

**Hardware Questions (H):**
- Equipment condition and testing
- Maintenance records
- Operational readiness

**Process Questions (P):**
- Procedures and their implementation
- SMS compliance
- Documentation review

## Preparation Checklist

### 30 Days Before Inspection

**Documentation:**
- [ ] Certificates validity check
- [ ] Training records updated
- [ ] Drills completed and logged
- [ ] Maintenance up to date
- [ ] Risk assessments current
- [ ] SMS familiarization complete

**Equipment:**
- [ ] All safety equipment tested
- [ ] Navigation equipment calibrated
- [ ] Cargo equipment operational
- [ ] Communication equipment tested
- [ ] Emergency equipment ready

### 7 Days Before

**Crew preparation:**
- [ ] Brief all officers on SIRE 2.0
- [ ] Review common questions
- [ ] Practice demonstrations
- [ ] Language preparation (English)
- [ ] Roles assigned

**Final checks:**
- [ ] Deck and accommodation housekeeping
- [ ] Engine room cleanliness
- [ ] Documentation organized
- [ ] Deficiencies rectified

### Day Before

- Final walkthrough
- Rest adequately
- Documents easily accessible
- Emergency equipment checked
- Crew confident and ready

## Key Areas of Focus

### Navigation

**Common questions:**
- Passage planning process
- ECDIS operation and updates
- AIS, radar, VDR operation
- Navigation in restricted visibility
- Bridge team management

**Preparation:**
- Know your equipment thoroughly
- Be able to demonstrate all functions
- Understand backup procedures
- BRM principles clear

### Cargo Operations

**Common questions:**
- Cargo plan understanding
- Tank cleaning procedures
- Inerting operations
- VOC management
- Enclosed space entry

**Preparation:**
- Know your current cargo
- Understand cargo characteristics
- Demonstrate procedures hands-on
- Safety measures clear

### Mooring Operations

**Common questions:**
- Mooring plan and risk assessment
- Line tending procedures
- Snap-back zone awareness
- PPE requirements
- Communication during mooring

**Preparation:**
- Walk through your mooring stations
- Know line specifications
- Understand emergency releases
- Team coordination clear

### Safety Equipment

**Common questions:**
- Lifeboat/raft operation
- Fire-fighting equipment
- SCBA donning and use
- Emergency procedures
- Rescue operations

**Preparation:**
- Be able to demonstrate equipment
- Know locations of all safety gear
- Understand maintenance schedules
- Emergency procedures memorized

## During the Inspection

### Best Practices

**Do:**
- Be honest and direct
- Say "I don't know" if unsure, then find out
- Demonstrate rather than describe
- Stay calm and professional
- Listen carefully to questions
- Engage the inspection team positively

**Don't:**
- Give answers you're not sure of
- Argue with inspector
- Make excuses
- Rush through demonstrations
- Leave inspector unattended
- Panic

### Handling Questions

**When asked to explain:**
1. Listen completely
2. Think before answering
3. Give structured response
4. Offer to demonstrate if possible
5. Ask if clarification needed

**When asked to demonstrate:**
1. Explain what you're about to do
2. Perform step-by-step
3. Point out safety features
4. Show you understand the "why"
5. Mention related procedures

## Post-Inspection

### Immediate Actions

1. Review inspector's comments
2. Begin rectification of observations
3. Document actions taken
4. Report to office promptly
5. Brief relieving officers

### Learning from Inspection

- Analyze observations thoroughly
- Update procedures if needed
- Share lessons with fleet
- Improve training
- Prepare for next inspection
